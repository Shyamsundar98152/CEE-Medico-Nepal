CEE Medico Nepal MEC Bachelor Level Common Entrance Examination 2026: Complete Guide to the Revised Syllabus The Medical Education Commission (MEC), Nepal, has officially released the revised Bachelor Level Common Entrance Examination Syllabus 2026. This syllabus serves as the foundation for admissions into various health science programs including MBBS, BDS, BSc Nursing, BPH, B Pharmacy, BPT, BAMS, BSc MLT, and several other allied health science courses. The latest revision, approved on April 28, 2026, introduces a more structured and detailed topic-wise framework for Physics, Chemistry, and Biology (PCB), helping students prepare more effectively for one of Nepal's most competitive entrance examinations. Background of the Common Entrance Examination Health professional education in Nepal has expanded significantly over the last several decades. With the growth of medical colleges and health science institutions, maintaining uniformity and quality in student selection became increasingly important. To address this challenge, the National Medical Education Act 2075 mandated the Medical Education Commission to conduct a common entrance examination for all bachelor-level health science programs. This system ensures transparency, fairness, and standardization across institutions throughout Nepal. Major Highlights of the 2026 Revision The third revision of the syllabus focuses primarily on strengthening the scientific foundation required for medical and health science education. Some of the key changes include: Detailed topic-wise content for Physics, Chemistry, Zoology, and Botany. Clear distribution of examination weightage. Better alignment with the latest Grade 11 and 12 science curriculum. Guidance for both students and question setters regarding the depth and scope of examination content. Continued emphasis on analytical thinking and application-based learning. Examination Pattern The entrance examination follows a standardized format: Total Questions: 200 MCQs Question Type: Single Best Response Options per Question: 4 Full Marks: 200 Negative Marking: 0.25 marks deducted for each incorrect answer Examination Duration: 3 Hours Qualifying Standard: 50th Percentile Difficulty Distribution: Recall Level: 50% Understanding Level: 30% Application Level: 20% This structure is designed to assess not only factual knowledge but also conceptual understanding and problem-solving ability.
