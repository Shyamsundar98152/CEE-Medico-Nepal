Topic: Chemical Kinetics (Chemistry)

For a zero-order chemical reaction, what happens to the half-life period ($t_{1/2}$) if the initial concentration of reactants is doubled?

Correct Answer: A (It is doubled) For a zero-order reaction, $t_{1/2} = [A]_0 / 2k$. Since the half-life is directly proportional to the initial concentration ($[A]_0$), doubling the initial concentration will double the half-life period.
